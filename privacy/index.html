Privacy Policy

This policy describes how forBET Media collects, uses, and protects information across our website and the digital products we operate.

Data controller: forBET Media (forBET Zakłady Bukmacherskie Sp. z o.o.), Warsaw, Poland
Contact: contact@forbetmedia.com
Last updated: April 2026

1. Introduction

forBET Zakłady Bukmacherskie Sp. z o.o. ("we", "us", "our") provides technology and media products including websites, mobile applications, and content platforms. We respect the privacy of every person who interacts with our products and we are committed to protecting personal information in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable laws.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices and rights available to you.

2. Information we collect

We collect only the information necessary to operate our products and respond to inquiries. Depending on how you interact with us, this may include:

  • Contact details you voluntarily provide, such as your name, email address, and any message content when you reach out to us.
  • Technical information automatically collected by our systems, such as device type, browser, language settings, approximate region, and the pages you visit.
  • Application data generated when you use our mobile or web applications, such as actions performed within the product, content viewed, and basic diagnostic data.
  • Cookies and similar identifiers for essential functionality and aggregated analytics, as described below.

We do not knowingly collect information from individuals who are not legally permitted to use our products.

3. How we use data

We process personal information for clearly defined purposes, including:

  • Operating, maintaining, and improving our websites and applications.
  • Responding to inquiries received through our contact channels.
  • Understanding aggregated usage patterns to improve product performance and content.
  • Ensuring the security, integrity, and reliability of our services.
  • Meeting legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

We do not sell personal information and we do not share it with third parties for unrelated marketing purposes.

4. Analytics

We use analytics tools to understand, in aggregate, how our products are used. Where possible we configure analytics to minimize personal data collection — for example by anonymizing IP addresses and avoiding cross-site tracking identifiers.

Analytics data is used to make product decisions and improve performance. It is not used to identify individual users.

5. Contact forms

When you send us a message via our website contact form or directly via email, the information you provide is used solely to respond to your inquiry. We retain this correspondence only as long as needed to address your request and meet related legal obligations.

6. Cookies

We use a limited set of cookies and similar technologies on our website:

  • Essential cookies required for the website to function (such as remembering user interface preferences).
  • Analytics cookies used to understand aggregated traffic patterns. These are used only with appropriate consent where required.

You can control cookies through your browser settings at any time. Disabling some cookies may affect the functionality of certain features.

7. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as needed for the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law. When information is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymized.

Retention periods vary depending on the type of data and the relevant legal obligations. Where appropriate, we apply automated deletion schedules.

8. Data sharing

We share personal data only when necessary to operate our services or as required by law. This may include:

  • Trusted service providers (such as hosting and analytics vendors) bound by appropriate data processing agreements.
  • Authorities, courts, or regulators when required by valid legal process.

All third parties handling personal data on our behalf are required to apply appropriate technical and organizational safeguards.

9. International transfers

Our primary infrastructure is hosted within the European Union. If any data transfer outside the EU is necessary, we apply the safeguards required under GDPR — including, where appropriate, Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent legal mechanisms.

10. Security

We apply technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include encryption in transit, restricted access controls, audit logging, and ongoing monitoring of our systems.

11. Your rights

Under GDPR and applicable laws, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to applicable legal exceptions.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing activities.
  • Request data portability where technically feasible.
  • Lodge a complaint with the data protection authority in your jurisdiction.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within the time periods required by applicable law.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, products, or legal requirements. Updated versions will be published on this page with a revised "Last updated" date.
